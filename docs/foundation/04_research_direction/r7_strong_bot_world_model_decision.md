# R7 Strong-Bot-Anchored Counterfactual World Model Decision

Status: active
Updated: 2026-04-26
Role: R7 frontier direction decision
Supersession rule: R7 replaces R6 as the active frontier plan. R0-R5 accepted
results remain frozen history. R6 remains a bounded historical frontier result,
but is no longer the active research carrier.

## One-Line Position

R7 does not continue belief gating on the weak self-built Protoss baseline.
R7 first acquires and audits a stronger bot or teacher substrate, then trains a
replay-grounded, action-conditioned counterfactual macro world model, and
validates it with offline, online, and external evidence.

## Why R7 Exists

The current self-built bot reached the planned core goal, but it is still a
minimal strategic system:

1. the accepted baseline is only Level 1 playable;
2. the accepted adaptive result is bounded and narrow;
3. the current action surface is too weak to make a stronger learned model
   strategically interesting;
4. a better predictor on top of a weak strategic substrate does not create a
   strong frontier claim;
5. the next meaningful step is to move the learned system onto a stronger
   substrate with richer decisions and better data.

## Historical Position of R6

R6 is not invalidated as repository history. It remains:

1. a completed bounded frontier result inside this repository;
2. proof that the project can build an offline-online-external research chain;
3. insufficient as the final long-term frontier carrier because the bot
   substrate is too weak.

R6 is therefore marked:

`completed_bounded_frontier_then_superseded_as_active_direction`

R7 must not rewrite checkpoint E, checkpoint F, or any accepted R6 checkpoint.

## Core Research Question

Given a stronger bot or teacher substrate, can we train a replay-grounded,
action-conditioned counterfactual macro world model that:

1. recovers hidden enemy state under partial observability;
2. predicts the consequence of meaningful macro actions;
3. improves online macro decisions over the strong bot alone or over a
   rule-based macro advisor;
4. shows positive evidence in an external bot ecosystem.

## Required Three-Part R7 Contribution

R7 only counts if these three pieces all exist together:

1. a strong bot or strong teacher substrate;
2. a replay-grounded counterfactual macro world model;
3. a macro action intervention surface.

Downloading a strong bot is not the contribution.
Training a model without online intervention is not the contribution.
Changing strong-bot rules without a learned model is not the contribution.

## Strong Bot Roles

A strong external asset may play one or more of these roles:

1. `substrate`
2. `teacher`
3. `comparator`

The role must be explicit, audited, and bounded by license and evidence rules.

## Allowed Macro Action Slate

The learned model must operate over meaningful macro actions such as:

1. `expand_now`
2. `delay_expand_and_defend`
3. `add_production`
4. `add_tech`
5. `add_detection`
6. `add_upgrade`
7. `shift_army_composition`
8. `increase_production_tempo`
9. `defensive_hold`
10. `move_out_window_open`
11. `delay_move_out`
12. `scout_or_rescout`
13. `counter_tech_response`
14. `punish_greed_response`

The final slate must be constrained by what the selected strong substrate can
actually expose or accept.

## What R7 Explicitly Forbids

1. no weak-bot frontier claim as the final carrier;
2. no use of downloaded strong-bot raw strength as our contribution;
3. no training before source and license audit;
4. no online claim without external validation;
5. no silent warm-start dependency becoming the main claim;
6. no hidden failed candidates;
7. no built-in-only frontier claim;
8. no accidental leakage from comparators into teacher training data.

## Success Definition

R7 is complete only if all of these become true:

1. a strong substrate or teacher source is acquired and audited;
2. strong replay or log data is materialized with labels and splits;
3. a counterfactual macro world model beats strong baseline floors offline;
4. model-driven intervention improves online play on the strong substrate;
5. external bot evaluation provides positive evidence;
6. every claim has exact evidence paths.

## Allowed External Claim After Completion

Only after R7 completes may we claim:

We built a strong-bot-anchored counterfactual macro world model for StarCraft
II. It uses strong replay data, predicts the consequence of strategically
meaningful macro actions under partial observability, and produces auditable
offline, online, and external gains.

## Claims That Remain Forbidden

Even if R7 completes, it does not automatically justify:

1. AlphaStar equivalence;
2. AI Arena top-tier strength;
3. universal generalization across all bots;
4. full end-to-end full-game learning;
5. credit for the raw strength of downloaded bots.
